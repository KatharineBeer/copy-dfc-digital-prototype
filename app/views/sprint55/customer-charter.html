National Careers Service Customer Charter

The National Careers Service helps you to realise your potential. We can help you make decisions on:

  • learning
  • training
  • work

The National Careers Service provides a free, impartial, high quality careers advice and guidance service. The National Careers Service is available to anyone aged 13 years old and over, living in England.

This Customer Charter tells you what to expect from us when you meet with an adviser.

You will:

  1. 1. get advice about how to use the service. This may be through the:
    • website
    • telephone advice line
    • local offices
  2. 2. speak to a qualified careers adviser who will consider your needs, interests and circumstances. They will give you useful, up to date information and advice. They can also help you make good choices about learning and work.
  3. 3. work with your adviser to make your meeting successful by:
    • keeping appointments
    • sharing information
    • being open to new ideas
    • participating fully
  4. 4. work with a careers adviser to agree a written action plan that sets out your next steps, if appropriate
  5. 5. find out about Apprenticeships and Traineeships to decide if they:
    • interest you
    • are a realistic option for you
  6. 6. find out how to contact other services that can help me if your adviser thinks they are appropriate
  7. 7. respond to requests to contact you in the future to:
    • support your progress
    • help improve the service
  8. 8. trust your careers adviser to keep an accurate record kept of your discussions. We respect and protect your personal information. We will not share your personal information outside the organisation without your consent
  9. 9. receive a response to your enquires within 3 working days. If you arrange an appointment, it will be at a convenient time and place
  10. 10. receive an apology and clear explanation if your appointment is delayed or cancelled
  11. 11. be able to comment on your satisfaction with the service. You may help improve the service by providing information about your progress and thoughts on the National Careers Service at a later date
  12. 12. know of the complaints procedure for the National Careers Service
  13. 13. receive acknowledgement to a complaint within 3 working days and a full response within 21 working days
