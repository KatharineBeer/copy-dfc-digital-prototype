View transcript

The National Careers Service provides high quality, free and impartial information advice and guidance to help citizens make decisions on learning, training and work. It is available to all adults in England over the age of 13.

Our universal service is primarily for citizens who need somewhere to go that can provide comprehensive careers information advice or guidance for themselves.

Additional users of our service are people who use it to support others. This includes professional careers advisers and intermediaries such as employers teachers and parents.

Our service is designed to enable users to self serve as much as possible, however some of our users need more intensive support.

For self service our intuitive digital tools and smart use of data enable them to get a personalised service. Professional careers advisers will help provide users with additional advice and guidance, specific to their personal circumstances.

If you want any more information about the National Careers Service, please get in touch.

Who is the National Careers Service for?

Citizens

We provide anyone aged 13+ with careers information, advice and guidance to help you make good career choices.

Intermediaries

We provide information for parents, teachers, coaches and advisers to support others in their career journey.

Stakeholders

We work with organisations and employers. We help promote the importance of careers guidance, taking into account labour market needs.

Telephone and web chat support

If you want some extra support on your career journey, you can contact us to speak to an adviser or start a web chat. Our careers advisers can help you work out where you want to go in your career and how to get there.

Personalised careers guidance

You can also access personalised careers guidance through the National Careers Service. Trained careers advisers can help you to build your confidence to take your next steps on your career journey.

If you contact an adviser for personalised careers guidance, our customer charter will help you understand what to expect from your meeting.
